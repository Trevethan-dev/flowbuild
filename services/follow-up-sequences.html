The problem

Warm leads go cold
because nobody
followed up enough.

Businesses spend a lot of money to acquire leads. A prospect fills out a form, books a call, attends a webinar, or downloads a resource. The business follows up once, maybe twice, and then moves on. Meanwhile, the prospect was genuinely interested — they just needed more time, or more information, or one more touchpoint before they were ready to say yes.

Research shows that 80% of sales require more than five follow-ups. Most sales people stop after one or two. That gap is where revenue disappears. And the painful part is that those leads were already warm — you'd already done the hard work of getting their attention. You just didn't stay in front of them long enough.

A well-built follow-up sequence is personalized at every step, pulls context from your CRM, and stops automatically the moment someone replies or books. It never feels like spam because it isn't.

How it works

Automated. Personalized.
Always on.

01

Trigger event fires

A lead fills out a form, books a call, attends a webinar, or hits any other trigger you define. The sequence kicks off within 60 seconds — while they're still thinking about you.

02

First message: instant & personal

A personalized message goes out immediately. It references what they did, what they'll get next, and sounds like it was written by a person — because the template was.

03

3–5 touch points over two weeks

Over the next two weeks, the system sends a series of value-driven follow-ups — spaced strategically, each pulling in fresh context from your CRM so they never feel templated.

04

Sequence stops on response

The moment someone replies or books a call, the sequence halts automatically. No awkward double-messages. No leads getting followed up after they've already said yes.

05

Sales team notified with full context

When a lead engages, your team gets notified with everything — the full conversation thread, their profile, what they clicked, and what they said. No digging required.
